Product Intelligence Template

A structured framework for explaining, comparing, and recommending products.

Why a Structured Template

Product research is often confusing. Descriptions vary wildly between sources. Reviews are subjective. Specifications are scattered. This makes it hard for buyers to compare options or make confident decisions.

Unstructured product descriptions lead to poor decisions ‚Äî buyers miss key details, overlook limitations, or choose based on incomplete information.

A fixed, consistent structure solves this. When every product is explained the same way, readers know exactly where to find what they need. It improves clarity, enables fair comparison, and builds trust.

This template focuses on decision-making ‚Äî helping people understand what a product is, who it's for, and whether it's right for them.

Core Template Sections

Each product report follows the same nine-section structure. Here's what each section covers:

Product Snapshot Quick overview: name, category, primary use, price range, and a one-line decision summary.
What This Product Is A clear, jargon-free explanation of what the product is and how it works.
Who It Is Useful For Specific audiences and use cases where this product makes sense ‚Äî and where it doesn't.
Types, Variants & Options Common variations, models, or configurations available in the market.
Features ‚Üí Practical Benefits Key features translated into real-world value ‚Äî what they actually mean for the buyer.
Pros, Cons & Limitations Honest assessment of strengths and weaknesses to set realistic expectations.
Comparison & Alternatives How this product compares to similar options in price, capability, and suitability.
Recommendation A clear summary of who should buy this, what to look for, and what to budget.
Where to Buy or Source Trusted retailers, suppliers, or sourcing options relevant to the product.

Reusability Across Products

This template is designed to work for any product category ‚Äî from consumer electronics to industrial equipment, sporting goods to packaging machinery.

  • The same structure applies whether the product costs ¬£20 or ¬£20,000
  • Enables consistent comparison between products in the same category
  • Reduces bias by asking the same questions for every product
  • Scales efficiently ‚Äî the same framework can cover thousands of products

Consistency Builds Trust

When users see the same structure across every report, they learn where to find answers quickly. This consistency makes Product Intelligence a reliable tool for decision-making.
